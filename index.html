Maryland Vaccine Dashboard

LAST UPDATED: MARCH 3, 2021

The Capital News Service Maryland COVID-19 Vaccine Dashboard is a resource for Marylanders to help navigate the sometimes confusing process of getting vaccinated. Below you will find information about who is currently eligible to be vaccinated and where to sign up for vaccinations in Maryland and the five most populated localities in the state. If you find any incorrect or outdated information on this site, please let us know.



How to get vaccinated in Maryland:



Maryland Vaccine Information

Who can get vaccinated

Maryland is currently in Phase 1C. This means that the following people are eligible to be vaccinated:

  • Anyone 65 and over, nursing home residents or staff.
  • All licensed and registered and certified healthcare providers.
  • Frontline hospital staff.
  • Law enforcement.
  • Firefighters.
  • EMS personnel.
  • Education staff (including K-12 teachers, support staff, and daycare providers).
  • Correctional healthcare staff and officers.
  • All public safety workers.
  • All healthcare workers.
  • Frontline judiciary staff.
  • Continuity of government workers.
  • Grocery store workers.
  • U.S.P.S. workers.
  • Agricultural production workers.
  • Public mass transit workers.
  • Critical manufacturing workers.
  • Veterinarians and veterinary support staff.
  • Clergy and other essential support for houses of worship.


More information about Maryland COVID-19 vaccine priority groups can be found here.

Maryland operates several mass vaccination centers, with more scheduled to open in the near future. Marylanders can also get vaccinated at several pharmacies operating across the state. Several hospitals and health providers are also providing vaccinations. All locations require appointments and preregistration.


Individuals can text “MDREADY” to 898-211 to receive text notifications on their smartphones regarding updates about Six Flags and the State’s other mass vaccination sites. Click here to text to receive updates.

Other important information:

  • Getting a vaccination at a local pharmacy can be difficult. Pharmacies like Walmart have not yet expanded into Maryland but are planning to do so in the near future. Others, like CVS, are inoculating Marylanders whenever vaccines are available but appointment slots fill up quickly.
  • Some hospitals have limited vaccinations, including Johns Hopkins Hospital, Greater Baltimore Medical Center, Sinai Hospital and others. Check Maryland’s vaccination clinic locator for participating hospital locations and individual hospital websites for more information.
  • Maryland plans to launch additional mass vaccination sites in the coming months, including one at M&T Bank Stadium in Baltimore scheduled for mid-February and plans for future locations in Western Maryland, Southern Maryland and the Eastern Shore are being finalized.

Baltimore City

Currently, Baltimore City is in Phase 1C. Information about priority groups can be found here. Individuals that fall under Phase 1A, 1B or 1C are eligible to register for a vaccine, including people 65 and older, healthcare workers, education staff and essential service employees. This includes Lord Baltimore TRI Center staff, Baltimore City Health Department Clinical Services and Syringe program staff, Convention Center Field Hospital staff and Baltimore City Health Department staff.

The Baltimore City Health Department vaccine is free, however, registration will ask for insurance information. People in groups 1A and 1B who come to a vaccine appointment must bring supporting documentation such as work ID, licensure or proof of employment.
