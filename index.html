Maryland Vaccine Information

Who can get vaccinated

Maryland is currently in Phase 1C. This means that the following people are eligible to be vaccinated: Anyone 65 and over, nursing home residents or staff, all licensed and registered and certified healthcare providers, frontline hospital staff, law enforcement, firefighters, EMS personnel, education staff (including K-12 teachers, support staff, and daycare providers), correctional healthcare staff and officers, all public safety workers, all healthcare workers, frontline judiciary staff, continuity of government workers, grocery store workers, U.S.P.S. workers, agricultural production workers, public mass transit workers, critical manufacturing workers, veterinarians and veterinary support staff, clergy and other essential support for houses of worship. More information about Maryland COVID-19 vaccine priority groups can be found here.

Maryland operates several mass vaccination centers, with more scheduled to open in the near future. Marylanders can also get vaccinated at several pharmacies operating across the state. Several hospitals and health providers are also providing vaccinations. All locations require appointments and pre-registration.

How to get vaccinated elsewhere in central Maryland:

State officials have provided some flexibility to local leaders to establish vaccine rollout guidelines in their county. As a result, some Maryland counties are in different rollout phases from the state. Below we examine who is eligible for a vaccine in Maryland’s most populous localities as well as other county-specific rules and vaccination locations. If you would like us to add your county to this site, please let us know.


Anne Arundel County

Currently, Anne Arundel County is giving appointments to those in Phase 1A and 1B. Information about priority groups can be found here. County public school staff and contractors will receive appointment invitations directly from Anne Arundel County Public School (AACPS) and do not have to preregister through the Health Department. Others included in Phases 1A and 1B should follow the link here to preregister. They will receive an email with available appointment times and locations once it’s the turn of their priority group.


Where to get vaccinated

Baltimore City

Currently, Baltimore City is in Phase 1C. Information about priority groups can be found here. Individuals that fall under Phase 1A, 1B or 1C are eligible to register for a vaccine, including people 65 and older, healthcare workers, education staff and essential service employees. This includes Lord Baltimore TRI Center staff, Baltimore City Health Department Clinical Services and Syringe program staff, Convention Center Field Hospital staff and Baltimore City Health Department staff.

The Baltimore City Health Department vaccine is free, however, registration will ask for insurance information. People in groups 1A and 1B who come to a vaccine appointment must bring supporting documentation such as work ID, licensure or proof of employment.


Where to get vaccinated

Baltimore County

Baltimore County is currently giving vaccines to those that qualify for Phase 1A and 1B. To find out what priority group you’re in, visit this site. To register for the vaccine from Baltimore County, complete this form and wait to be contacted by county staff for instructions on how to make an appointment.


Where to get vaccinated

Montgomery County

Montgomery county is currently taking appointments for individuals in Phase 1A and Tier 1 of Phase 1B. Individuals in the other tiers of 1B and 1C are able to pre-register for appointments now. Information about priority groups can be found here.

Pre-register for appointments from Montgomery County here. Patients will be contacted with a link to schedule appointments and available times, dates and clinics. For help filling out the online form, call 240-777-2982.

Sign-up for text and email updates about vaccines here. To find out what to expect at your vaccination appointment, visit this site. Click here for frequently asked questions about vaccines from Montgomery County.


Where to get vaccinated

Prince George's County

Prince Georges County is currently providing vaccinations to individuals in Phase 1A, 1B and 1C who live or work in Prince George’s County. Information about priority groups can be found here.

Vaccines are given by appointment only and everyone must pre-register to be vaccinated at this link. Pre-registered individuals will receive an email from Prince George’s Health Department when appointments become available. COVID-19 vaccines are free for all residents who live and work in Prince George’s County regardless of insurance coverage or immigration status.
